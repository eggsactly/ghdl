GHDL

where VHDL meets gcc

GHDL is the most popular free and open-source compiler and simulator for the VHDL language, i.e., it allows you to analyse, elaborate and execute VHDL code directly in a CPU-based computer. With its wide language standard support and amazing speed, GHDL makes designing and testing hardware systems available to everyone.

Wide language standard support
Fully 1987, 1993 and 2002; partially 2008
Get an executable binary from your design
Multiple backend support: LLVM, GCC or, x86_64/i386 only, a built-in one.
Blistering Speed
An executable binary is much faster than any interpreted simulator.
Available on multiple platforms
Has been successfully built on GNU/Linux, Windows™, macOS™ and FreeBSD, on x86, x86_64 and PowerPC.
Write waveforms to multiple formats
GHW, VCD or FST files can be exported for visual inspection with a waveform viewer.
Explore your design
GHDL can be used to pretty print or to generate cross references in HTML.
Call a foreign language
Since GHDL is a compiler, you can call functions or procedures written in a foreign language, such as C, C++ or Ada95.
Support of extension specifications and interfaces.
Partial support of PSL, VPI interface and annotation of designs by means of a SDF file.
Support of third party projects
VUnit, OSVVM, cocotb (through the VPI interface), …
GHDL can be used, modified and re-distributed. If you are interested in getting involved in current GHDL development, do not hesitate to contact us!
Contribute!
VHDL standards are carefully implemented. Indeed, GHDL has been reported to have a better implementation of standards than some commercial simulators. Yet, there might be bugs.
